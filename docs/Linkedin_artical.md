Understanding Cost-to-Serve in the Canadian Context 
Cost-to-serve represents the total expense utilities incur to deliver electricity reliably to customers, encompassing everything from generation and transmission to customer service and regulatory compliance. For Canadian utilities, this metric has become increasingly critical as provinces like Ontario project 75% electricity demand growth through 2050, while aging infrastructure and extreme weather events strain operational efficiency.

Understanding Cost-to-Serve Cost-to-serve is physics plus process: energy moved, assets maintained, customers served, rules obeyed. In Canada, distance and climate amplify every line item. Stop treating these as fate; treat them as variables to optimize. Measure everything. Automate everything that’s repetitive.

Canada's electricity system operates under unique constraints that drive higher operational costs. The country maintains over 160,000 kilometers of high-voltage transmission lines, serving a population density of just 4 people per square kilometer across often harsh terrain. This geographic reality, combined with aging infrastructure and regulatory complexity, creates significant cost pressures. powermag

Key Cost Drivers in Canadian Utilities:

Geographic Scale: Transmission and distribution costs average CAD $45/MWh compared to $29/MWh in the US
