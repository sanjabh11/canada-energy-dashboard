# COURSE 1: RATE BASICS

## Chapter 1: Electricity Pricing Fundamentals

### Lesson 1.1: Understanding Electricity Pricing in Canada
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** Understanding Electricity Pricing in Canada

**Content:**
```
Canada has different electricity pricing systems across provinces:

• Alberta: Deregulated market - prices change every 5 minutes based on supply/demand
• Ontario: Time-of-use pricing - rates vary by time of day
• Quebec, BC, Manitoba: Regulated prices - set by government/utilities

In Alberta, the Alberta Electric System Operator (AESO) operates the grid and manages the wholesale electricity market. This is what makes Alberta unique!

KEY CONCEPT: The "pool price" in Alberta can range from $0 to $999/MWh depending on conditions.
```

### Lesson 1.2: What is the Rate of Last Resort (RoLR)?
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** What is the Rate of Last Resort (RoLR)?

**Content:**
```
The Rate of Last Resort (RoLR) is a regulated rate for consumers who don't choose a competitive electricity retailer. It replaced the old Regulated Rate Option (RRO) on January 1, 2025.

OFFICIAL RoLR RATES (Jan 2025 - Dec 2026):

Provider          | Service Area    | Rate
------------------|-----------------|------------
EPCOR             | Edmonton        | 12.01¢/kWh
Direct Energy     | Southern AB     | 12.02¢/kWh
ENMAX             | Calgary         | 12.06¢/kWh

*Approved by AUC Decision 29204-D01-2024*

KEY RULES:
• Fixed for 2 years
• After 2 years: maximum 10% adjustment
• Includes 0.1¢/kWh Consumer Awareness Surcharge

WHY IT MATTERS:
If you're on RoLR, you're paying 3-4¢/kWh MORE than competitive retailers. That's $200-300/year extra!
```

### Lesson 1.4: Understanding Your Electricity Bill
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** Understanding Your Electricity Bill

**Content:**
```
Your electricity bill has multiple components:

1. ENERGY CHARGE - Cost of the electricity you use (this is what varies by retailer)
2. TRANSMISSION - Moving electricity from generators to your area
3. DISTRIBUTION - Local delivery to your home
4. RATE RIDERS - Adjustments for under/over-collected costs
5. ADMIN FEE - Monthly fixed charge from retailer

PRO TIP: The energy charge is the only part you can reduce by switching retailers. Transmission and distribution are regulated and the same for everyone.
```

# COURSE 2: RESIDENTIAL ENERGY OPTIMIZATION

## Chapter 1: Grid Operations & Case Studies

### Lesson 2.1: How Alberta's Grid Works
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** How Alberta's Grid Works

**Content:**
```
Alberta operates an energy-only deregulated electricity market where:

1. GENERATORS compete to sell electricity
2. POOL PRICE is set every 5 minutes based on supply/demand
3. AESO manages grid reliability and market operations
4. RETAILERS buy from the pool and sell to consumers

KEY CONCEPT: MERIT ORDER
Generators are "stacked" in order of their offered price. The cheapest run first. The most expensive unit needed to meet demand sets the pool price for everyone.

This means:
• Low-cost renewables often set low prices
• Gas plants set the price most hours
• During emergencies, highest-cost units set $999/MWh prices
```

# COURSE 4: TIER COMPLIANCE

## Chapter 1: Industrial Carbon Pricing

### Lesson 4.1: What is TIER?
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** What is TIER?

**Content:**
```
TIER = Technology Innovation and Emissions Reduction regulation

Alberta's industrial carbon pricing system for large emitters:
• Applies to facilities emitting 100,000+ tonnes CO2e/year
• Smaller facilities (10,000-100,000) can opt-in
• Benchmark + output-based system

KEY CONCEPT:
Facilities that emit below their benchmark earn credits. Those above must pay or buy credits.

The TIER system covers approximately 60% of Alberta's total emissions, including:
• Oil sands operations
• Power generation
• Natural gas processing
• Petrochemicals
• Mining operations
```

---

### Lesson 4.2: Benchmark Calculations
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** Benchmark Calculations

**Content:**
```
Each industrial sector has an emissions benchmark (tonnes CO2e per unit of output).

EXAMPLE - OIL SANDS:
• Benchmark: 0.15 tonnes CO2e per barrel
• Your facility produces 10M barrels and emits 1.6M tonnes
• Benchmark limit: 10M × 0.15 = 1.5M tonnes
• Excess: 100,000 tonnes requiring compliance

BENCHMARK TIGHTENING SCHEDULE:
• Most facilities: 2% per year reduction
• Oil sands: 4% per year (starting 2029)
• Working toward 90% tightening by 2030

HIGH-PERFORMANCE BENCHMARKS (HPBs):
• Top 10% performers in each sector
• Facilities below HPB earn EPCs
• Used for compliance credit generation
```

### Lesson 4.4: DIC Formula & Credit Reactivation
**Type:** Multimedia (Text)

**Title:** 2025 Amendments - DIC Formula & Credit Reactivation

**Content:**
```
The December 2025 TIER amendments (Order in Council 369/2025):

1. DIRECT INVESTMENT CREDITS (DIC) - THE FORMULA

    Investment Credits = Eligible Investment ÷ $95

EXAMPLE: $9.5 million investment ÷ $95 = 100,000 credits

KEY RULES:
• Can cover up to 90% of compliance obligation (2026+)
• Must use within 5 years
• Non-transferable (cannot sell to others)
• Applies to compliance years 2026+

COMPLIANCE LIMIT PROGRESSION:
Year   | Max Credits/Offsets/EPCs
-------|-------------------------
2024   | 70%
2025   | 80%
2026+  | 90%

2. CREDIT REACTIVATION ("ZOMBIE CREDITS")

🚨 Major strategic opportunity!

THE RULE: If you invest in a DIC project, you can "un-retire" credits from 2023-2024.

REQUIREMENTS:
• Submit audited investment statement by March 31
• Only credits retired in preceding 3 years eligible
• No earlier than 2025 baseline

MARKET IMPACT:
• 24 million tonnes of surplus credits = 3 years of demand!
• Companies with retired credit banks have hidden assets
```
