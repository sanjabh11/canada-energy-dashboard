## Page 4 — Phase 2 Gate Assessment

### Adversarial Review: "Why would the TIER beachhead fail?"

| Challenge | Response | Risk level |
|---|---|---|
| "TIER facilities already have compliance consultants" | CEIP is a tool FOR the compliance lead, not a replacement for consultants. Consultants can use CEIP to serve more clients faster. | LOW |
| "Targray or cCarbon could add facility-level tools" | Targray is a trader (revenue from credit sales, not software). cCarbon is free and macro-focused. Neither has incentive to build facility-level CFO memos. | LOW |
| "Facilities could build this in Excel" | They already do — but it's error-prone, not source-dated, and doesn't produce board-ready exports. CEIP's value is provenance + format + speed. | MEDIUM |
| "TIER regulation could change or be repealed" | TIER has statutory review by Dec 31, 2026 but Alberta has committed to carbon pricing. Even if thresholds change, compliance tools remain needed. | LOW-MEDIUM |
| "No buyer evidence exists — 0 pilot proof" | True — this is the #1 risk. Phase 3 will address evidence gap remediation. | HIGH |
| "Orennia has SOC 2 and 153 engineers" | Orennia serves a different buyer (investors/developers). SOC 2 is not required for a compliance memo tool. CEIP's security procurement pack addresses buyer security concerns. | LOW |
